## Class Pulse: A Presentation of Learning
### Strengthening Schools Project — FY26

# The Hook

There's a student in almost every classroom.

She follows along. She copies the notes. She never raises her hand — not because she understands, but because she'd rather be invisible than wrong.

**We built Class Pulse for her.**

# The Problem

**Every 5th-grade math classroom has a moment we can't see:**
The moment a student gets lost — and decides not to tell anyone.

By the time we discover that confusion, through a quiz, a grade, a behavior incident:

> The lesson has moved on.
> The gap has grown.
> The moment is gone.

<!-- VISUAL: Simple timeline graphic — "Student gets confused" → gap → "Teacher finds out." Emphasize the distance. -->

---

# Who This Happens To Most

This silence does not fall equally.

Students who are already furthest from grade level are the least likely to ask for help — and the most likely to interpret confusion as a personal failure.

**What should be a temporary gap becomes a permanent identity:**
*"I'm just not a math person."*

<!-- VISUAL: Two-column layout. Left: photo of a classroom in motion. Right: text only. Clean split. -->

---

# Our Context

We teach in a system where:

- ✗ Intervention happens *after* the lesson, not *during* it
- ✗ Teachers learn who was lost from the *next quiz*, not the current moment
- ✗ The loudest signal of confusion is often a *behavior incident*

**This is not a failure of teachers. It is a failure of tools.**

<!-- VISUAL: Three bullet points with subtle red "x" icons. Clean white text on dark background. -->

# The Insight

What if a teacher could know — *in the middle of the lesson* — exactly who was lost?

What if that student didn't have to raise their hand?

What if support arrived in **60 seconds** instead of tomorrow?

**That is not a fantasy. That is what we set out to build.**

| Value | What It Means |

| **Privacy as Promise** | Student data is a sacred trust |

| **Equity in the Moment** | Every student deserves to be reached *right now* |

# Section 2: The Solution

---

# What Class Pulse Does — Simply Put

A student doesn't understand today's lesson.

Instead of staying silent, she taps a button on her device.
Her teacher's screen shows a quiet signal — no name, no spotlight.
Within 30 seconds, the teacher speaks a single targeted sentence that reaches her.

**She gets it. She signals green. She stays in math.**

*That is Class Pulse. That is the whole thing.*

<!-- VISUAL: Simple illustrated flow: student taps → signal appears → teacher responds → green light. Icon-style, not technical. -->

---

# How We Built It — The Human Version

We did not build this at a desk.

We built it in classrooms.
Watching how teachers move when they're mid-lesson.
Watching which students never raise their hands.
Asking students what would make it feel safe to say: *"I don't get it."*

**Every design decision came from that room. Not from a screen.**

- **The TFA SSP community** — who held us accountable to each other

# Our Goals — In Plain Language

**Goal 1:** Students get support *during* the lesson — not after — 80%+ of the time.

**Goal 2:** Teachers have a 30-second, standards-linked coaching script the moment they need one — with zero prep.

**Goal 3:** This practice outlasts us — any teacher, any classroom, same results.

<!-- VISUAL: Three goals as large numbered statements. Each with a short supporting line. Airy layout. -->

---

# How We Knew If It Was Working

We monitored three things — weekly:

1. **Signal usage** — Are students actually tapping the button?
2. **Intervention speed** — How fast is the teacher responding?
3. **Teacher reflection** — Do they feel like fewer students are falling through?

We adjusted every week.
We never stopped asking: *Is this reaching her?*

# Section 3: What We Found

---

# The Numbers

When teachers had real-time comprehension data, everything shifted:

- **80%+** of students actively used the signal by Week 4
- **30% more** Checks for Understanding completed daily
- Intervention time dropped from **next day → under 60 seconds**
- Teachers delivered targeted support an average of **8 times per class period** that previously went unaddressed

# What We Saw in Students

Students stopped *performing* understanding.

They started *signaling* confusion — quietly, safely, honestly.

A 10-year-old who trusts that asking for help won't hurt them is on a different trajectory. Not just in math. In everything.

**We did not manufacture that trust. We built the conditions for it.**

# The Takeaway

The tool we built will keep improving.

But the most enduring thing we built is a *practice*:

**The habit of asking — in the middle of the lesson, not at the end —
who is lost right now, and what do I do about it?**

Teachers who used Class Pulse now ask that question differently.
Students now expect to be reached in time.

*That shift cannot be unbuilt.*
